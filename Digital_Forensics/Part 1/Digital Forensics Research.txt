Digital Forensics is basically, examining artifacts from a digital device. Artifacts can include images, videos, emails, SMSs (text messages), documents, deleted files, and more.

The Digital Forensics Research Workshop (DFRWS) consists of several organizations bringing about a set of standards to the field.  DFRWS defines the six phases of the digital forensic process. Click on each phase for more information.

==Identification
	the first stage indentifies potential sources of evidence as well as the key custonians and location of data.

==Preservation
	The process of preserving any electronically stored information ESI.  This includes imaging technologies documentation and chain of custody to ensure an accurate and acceptable preservation of the evidence.


==Collection
	involves collecting devices and data based on approved methods

==Examination
	An indepth systemic search of the evidence related to the case.

==Analysis
	Analysis aims to draw conclusions based on the evidence found during the examination.

==Presentation
	Report writing and presentation of results.
	
	


Three types of data

==Data in Use

Active data currently being used - spreadsheets, Word documents, e
	

==Data in Flight

Data that is actively moving from one device to another like in a network or to and from the cloud.


==Data at rest

Data at rest examinations involve data that is stored on a local machine. That local machine can be a computer, server, mobile device, IoT device, or even an automobile. It means data that is stored and not data that is actively moving from one device to another like in a network or to and from the cloud.
